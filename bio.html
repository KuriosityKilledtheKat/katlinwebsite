Kat Lin is a nonfiction writer and experimental storyteller.

She defected from the world of biomedical engineering after graduating with a Bachelor's in it. She couldn't empathize well with the HUVEC cells she had to work with. Instead, she freelances as a writer.

She has words in the New Yorker online and runs a photography business.

Her awards include a national semifinalist award for the Norman Mailer Writing Competition.
